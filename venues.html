Where to Go

Here is a sample of some venues to check out out in the Phoenix Metro Area.

Venues
Name Address Description Details
Club Red 1306 W. University Dr. Mesa, AZ. 85201 Large capacity all-ages venue Originally a small movie theater. Two large rooms. Full bar. Excellent cooling system. Large bathrooms. Only open for events. No kitchen. Plenty of parking.
Marquee Theater 730 N. Mill Ave. Tempe AZ. 85281 Extra large all ages concert hall. Designed as a music hall. Full bar. Large bathrooms. Only open for events. No kitchen. Floor slants towards stage-wear comfortable shoes. Must pay to park, Light rail accessible.
Joe’s Grotto 13825 N. 32nd St. Phoenix, AZ. 85032 Large sized bar. Hosts some all-ages shows. This is a bar that hosts metal shows. It is open on non-event nights. No kitchen. Might have to cut through a mosh-pit to use the small bathroom. Decent parking if you arrive early.
The Pressroom 441 W. Madison St. Phoenix, AZ. 85003 Medium sized all-ages concert venue. Originally a print shop, Gets muggy. Bathroom set-up: Mens toilets, ladies toilets, communal sinks. Full bar. No kitchen. Light-rail accessible and recommended.
Pub Rock 8005 E. Roosevelt St. Scottsdale, AZ. 85257 Medium sized bar. Hosts some all-ages shows. Full bar. Open everyday. No kitchen. Excellent parking.
Rogue Bar 423 N. Scottsdale Rd. Scottsdale, AZ 85257 Small sized bar. 21+ venue. Dive bar where you can get up close and personal with the musicians. Full bar. No kitchen. Can park in neighborhood if needed. Open every day.
Tempe Tavern 1810 E. Apache Blvd. Tempe, AZ 85281 Small sized bar. 21+ venue. Minimal parking, light rail accessible. Close interaction with bands. Full bar. Full kitchen. Open everyday.
Yucca Tap Room 29 W. Southern Ave. Tempe, AZ. 85282 Medium sized bar. 21+ venue. Excellent parking. Full bar. Full kitchen. Close interaction with bands. Usually no cover charge. Open everyday.
